Anti-Aging Research Mind Map

An interactive map of the most promising avenues of human longevity research — the biological levers scientists are pulling to slow, stop, or reverse aging — connected to the researchers driving them and the landmark studies, books, and films behind each idea. Click any node to read a summary, a deeper explanation, quick facts, source excerpts, and (where one exists) a Wikipedia link. Drag nodes to rearrange, scroll to zoom, and drag the background to pan.

Drag · scroll to zoom · click a node

Select a node to see a summary, a deeper explanation, quick facts, source excerpts, and connections. Start with the blue hub in the center, or a green research avenue around it — or use the filters to isolate people, papers, books, or films.

Studies link to their DOI (permanent); books and some resources link to a web search or official page; every node with a Wikipedia article links to it. This map is a personal learning tool, not medical advice — many interventions shown are supported chiefly by animal or early human data, and a few (e.g. taurine, young-blood factors) remain contested. Framework grounded in López-Otín et al., “Hallmarks of Aging: An Expanding Universe,” Cell (2023).
